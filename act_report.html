Introduction¶

For this project I got the chance to work with WeRateDogs Twitter account that gathers and comments on funny dog pictures. Part of my analysis involved number of retweets vs likes, what users are most likely to retweet and what breed is the most popular within the virtual community.

Overall analysis and results visualization¶

I start first with a heat map. Using this type of visualization method I was able to have an overview of the relationship between variables where the higher the coefficient is, the stronger the relationship. It is clear that in my case the number of likes(favorites) and the number of retweets have a stong possitive correlation. Which means that as the retweets go up so do the likes. But for my suprise there was quite a big gap between these two types of reactions. There are more likes than retweets. So I went ahead and try to find why.

Once I noticed that there are 12,144,577 more likes then retweet I looked more into that and what could cause it. I wanted to check if the divice of the user is related to that. I query dataset by the source type ( TweetDeck, Iphone and web client) and found the mean of the retweets. What I discovered and that also shows in the pie plot below is that users using iphone to browse on WeRateDogs are more likely to retweet, than the rest of the users.
